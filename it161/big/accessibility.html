Accessibility

Accessibility is a big part of web design and development. Developing just a working website is not enough. There are different features and attributes to a website that makes it better quality compared to other websites, and a major one of them is accessibility. Each year web design standards get stricter and more inclusive of the disabled, and more countries are adopting those standards. Instead of being optional, website accessibility is mandatory, because access to information and communications technologies, including the Web, is defined as a basic human right in the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UN CRPD).(w3.org) Also, it is a deciding factor of a successful website!

Unfortunately, there are still a lot of websites that don't have or have enough accessibility features. Those are usually smaller websites, because big corporations or government websites must have decent accessibility or they might even get fined! For example, recent changes to laws in Canada mean that a corporation without an accessible website could face daily fines of $100,000 with company directors also being fined to the tune of $50,000 per day.(Digital Design Standards) Moreover, Section 508 is the governing principle and it requires that all government information be accessible to disabled users. (usability.gov)

Although most internet users are healthy and not disabled, according to Web AIM, some 20% of the world’s population suffers from a disability, websites that aren’t accessible could exclude millions of people from getting the information they need. (Digital Design Standards) Further, disability can happen to anyone and there are cases that even non-disbaled people need accessibility features, such as people using mobile phones, smart watches, smart TVs, and other devices with small screens, different input modes, etc., older people with changing abilities due to ageing, people with “temporary disabilities'' such as a broken arm or lost glasses, people with “situational limitations'' such as in bright sunlight or in an environment where they cannot listen to audio, people using a slow Internet connection, or who have limited or expensive bandwidth.(w3.org) Making your website accessible is not only a good business practice, but it also shows people you care!
